I am an astronomer and NHFP Hubble Fellow in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of California, Davis. I received my Ph.D. in Astronomy in 2018 from UCLA, where I worked with Alice Shapley, and did my undergraduate work in physics at the University of Louisville. I research the evolution of galaxies over cosmic time, and am particularly interested in how gas moves through galaxies and fuels their growth, the chemical abundances of stars and gas and what they can tell about galaxy formation histories, and the properties of massive stars and the gas that they ionize.

My hobbies include music, the outdoors, fitness, reading, and Nintendo. At Louisville, I completed a music performance degree in jazz saxophone. I also play piano and flute, and occasionally like to have fun with synthesizers. I enjoy classical and jazz music in addition to a wide variety styles and genres that does not exclude popular songs and bands. I love to hike, backpack, and climb mountains. The photo on my homepage is from a fall backpacking trip to Yosemite, while the picture to the right is at the summit of Redcloud Peak, a 14,034' mountain in Colorado. To stay fit, you can find me running or working out at Newton's Law of Fitness on most days. I enjoy reading books on a variety of topics in fiction and non-fiction. I'm a huge J.R.R. Tolkien fan, and also love Ray Bradbury. Recently finished books include Dante Alighieri's Divine Comedy, Invisible Man by Ralph Ellison, Foundation by Isaac Asimov, and Joseph Conrad's Heart of Darkness. My favorite line of video games is Nintendo's Metroid franchise, followed closely by Zelda.
